Fish

What are fish?

Fishes were some of the the first vertebrates to evolve from invertebrate ancestors. The earliest known
fishes were the ostracoderms, a now-extinct group of jawless fishes that appeared in the Cambrian
Period, about 510 million years ago. Other early fish include the conodonts and the agnanthans (the
hagfish and the lamprey). Fishes later evolved jaws and diversified into a number of lineages including
cartilaginous fishes, ray-finned fishes, and lobe-finned fishes. There are an estimated 25,000 species of
fishes alive today.
