Invertebrates

What are invertebrates?

Invertebrates were the first animals to evolve, and they did so from single-celled microorganisms. Fossil
evidence of early invertebrates dates back to the late Precambrian, 600 million years ago. Since that time,
invertebrates have diversified into countless forms. A staggering 97 percent of all species alive today
are invertebrates. As a group, invertebrates are united more by what they lack (a backbone) than by
shared characteristics.
